Yellow Tulip

After watching all the rose/lily-modeling tutorials on youtube, modeling my own tulip becomes much easier. I like the little detail of the petal shape and the stamen:

I told Phil I don’t like the lighting effect and we decided to adjust the material and render settings.

Delete omni light and add an area light:

Add Ambient Occlusion in my render setting. I don’t really like it:

Add Global Illumination in my render setting, the shadow becomes much softer. The petal’s material is a little bit transclucent, there are light coming through the petals that we can tell the transclucent. It’s much better:

Add subsurface scattering as texture of luminance in material. It gives the flower a more wax-like look. But the color becomes too white:

Delete the color and just use luminance to colorize the petal. It’s too dark now:

Add a little bit color with the luminance. And render it with global illumination. So far this is my favorite:

Add a water texture of bump in material. It adds some petal vein. I’m not a fan to realistic style so this doesn’t pleasant me that much:
